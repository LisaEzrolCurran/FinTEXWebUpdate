What is FinTech?

FinTech has come to be shorthand for technology enabled innovation in financial services. The roots of the term FinTech come from the days when automated back-office systems were cutting edge, and FinTech stood for Financial Technology – the technologies employed by large financial institutions to modernize their operations. Today, FinTech is much more.

Now, FinTech refers to everything that changes the way money is handled and financial transactions are completed. It’s a systems employed by a large financial institutions, payment apps on consumer’s phone, startup created platforms that facilitate new lending models. FinTech has moved way beyond the back office to being highly visible, having direct impact on the day to day lives of millions of people.

What is a Business Cluster?

A business cluster is a group of interconnected companies, suppliers, service providers and associated institutions in an industry that are located within close geographic proximity. Michael Porter of Harvard Business School published a landmark study on the competitive advantage of clusters. He proposed clusters create three advantages:

    Productivity
  • Better access to employees and suppliers
  • Access to specialized information
  • Access to institutions and public goods
